1. Cover Page

Show me a specialist, and I’ll show you a man who’s so scared he’s dug a hole for himself to hide in.

Kurt Vonnegut - Player Piano

Here's where people usually define the kind of work that they do in pithy fashion, big font, above a pretty picture ("I love to develop beautiful applications", "I love to write clean, well documented code", etc...).

I used to do the same until I realized that I was constantly pidgeonholing myself into particular roles, which usually led to disappointing outcomes (for me). I've worked as a web developer, visual and UX designer, and a print designer. I've worked on my own products and started my own small businesses. I've made mistakes along the way; learned a lot in doing so.

The lack of clarity above is intentional; here is where I might interject a little and hone in on what I actually do.

I like problem solving. Throughout my career that has involved a number of different tools and roles. I can play an engineer or a designer as needed. I can talk business strategy and look at the big picture. I like to work in the early stages of startups. I like to move quickly, and I really, __really__ dislike when process trumps results.

I will make no claims at being the best at any of these things. But usually, I'm pretty good.

If you need someone who can take on a variety of roles, and switch contexts quickly between detailed views of a project and the big, global view, we should probably talk.

2. About

I got my undergraduate degree from the University of Southern California, where I studied Psychology. During school, I worked on a variety of design projects, and tried to start a non-profit and a startup at the same time. I think you might guess where both of those projects went.

In 2012, I signed up for a program called App Academy, where one could learn how to become a web developer. Seeing nothing better to do, I applied, got accepted, and took my LA life to the Bay Area, where I currently live still.

I'm an easy person to work and get along with.

5. Colophon

This portfolio was "typeset" in Times New Roman for header and link elements. The body text is Inconsolata (in 14pt), a monospace font, frequently used in code editors.

The code for the site was written in Sublime Text 2, in plain HTML, CSS, and Javascript. The jQuery library is used for some interactions and resizing to screens.

The hosting is graciously provided by Github pages.

I don't care if this site works in IE8, 9, or 10. I care a little if it doesn't work in 11.
